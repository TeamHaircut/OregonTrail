River Crossing

To ford a river means to pull your wagon across a
shallow part of the river, with the oxen still attached.

To caulk the wagon means to seal it so that
no water can get in. The wagon can then be
floated across like a boat.

To use a ferry means to put your wagon on top
of a flat boat that belongs to someone else.
The owner of the ferry will take your wagon
across the river for a price, however you might
have to wait a couple of days.
